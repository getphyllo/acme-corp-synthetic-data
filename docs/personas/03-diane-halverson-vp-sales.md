## Who she is

Diane is the best Sales VP Acme has had in 20 years. She came up at P&G in the brand-marketing side before crossing over to sales — a relatively rare profile that gives her the ability to switch between commercial and brand language fluently. She moved to Post Foods in 2008 and rose through the East Region. Whitfield recruited her in 2018 as one of his early bets on the Acme turnaround.

She is calm in a way that makes other people calmer. She walks into a room of frustrated NAMs and 30 minutes later they have a plan. She does not raise her voice. She does not have to.

She is the **economic buyer for any Sales- or Insights-side enterprise platform** — Marcus, Tom Reilly, Priya, even Robert Kim's team will defer to her sign-off on tools that show up in their workflows. She is the most likely Clayface signer if the deal is positioned commercially.

She is also tired. Not in a complaining way; in a deep, structural way. The trade-pullback transformation, the Walmart facing fight, the Louisiana decline, the ProteinPeak distribution push — all of it lands in her week. She has been doing this for 27 years. She is starting to think about what the last 7 years of her career look like.

## Personality in three lines

- Calm authority. Takes notes by hand. Listens twice as much as she speaks.
- Translator between commercial and marketing. The only person at Acme who can defend a trade-spend decision to Audrey AND a brand-equity decision to Nathan in the same meeting.
- Has a deeply personal sense of customer relationships. Walks into a Kroger JBP and remembers the buyer's mother's name.

## What Diane is paid to do

Hit the North America revenue number. Lead the sales organization. Defend distribution at the strategic accounts. Develop the NAM and regional director bench. Translate Sales reality up to the C-suite and CEO clearly to the Sales org. Buy the right tools and shut down the wrong ones.

## Her recurring pains

1. **Visibility lag.** The cycle from "something changed in the market" → "Sales hears about it" → "we have a plan" is too long. The Louisiana case is a perfect example.
2. **The trade pullback / distribution defense paradox.** Whitfield wants margin. Sales needs trade. The middle path is always uncomfortable.
3. **Tool fragmentation in her org.** Every NAM has their own retailer-specific platform plus internal sales reporting. There is no synthesis layer.
4. **The board narrative.** She has to explain a 340bps regional decline in a way that doesn't sound like an excuse, while also being honest about what's happening.
5. **Talent retention.** Marcus is being recruited by a competitor (she suspects). Tom Reilly turns 52 next year. Soo-jin Lee at Target is destined for bigger things — at Acme or elsewhere.
6. **Time.** She is the bottleneck on at least 30 decisions a week.

## What she wants from a Clayface-shaped tool (in her own words)

- *"I want a Monday morning headline. Not a dashboard. A sentence I can read on my phone before yoga."*
- *"I want my regional directors to walk into Friday business reviews already aligned on what changed. I don't want to be the synthesis layer."*
- *"I want my insights team to spend less time pulling and more time thinking. I will pay for that."*
- *"I want to know what a buyer at Rouses is going to ask my NAM next Tuesday before they ask it."*
- *"I want one platform, not eleven. If you can replace four of my tools, I will write the check."*

## How she shows up in a buying cycle

- Will not sit through a feature demo. Will sit through a 20-minute strategic conversation.
- Wants a one-page business case with consolidation savings and the executive output.
- Wants the platform to make Maya's day easier and Marcus's life simpler.
- The conversation she will fund: *"This replaces four line items, saves us $600K, gives Maya 5 hours back per week, and gives Marcus a 60-day-earlier signal on regional shifts."*
- Will check with Robert Kim and Nathan Reeves before signing.
- Approval threshold: ~$500K is hers to sign with Nathan. Above $500K needs Whitfield read.

## What loses Diane

- Vendor-speak ("AI-native," "agentic," "co-pilot")
- A demo where the second screen is harder to navigate than the first
- Anything that suggests Acme should restructure its team to use the tool
- Anything implied by "if you connect 14 data sources..." (she has 14 data sources because integration is hard)

## Her relationship with the other three personas

- **With Maya (Persona 1):** Has personally approved Maya's promotion path. Mentioned her by name in a recent CMO update. Will protect her in any leadership context.
- **With Marcus (Persona 2):** Her direct report. Trusts him. Currently watching closely how he handles Louisiana.
- **With Priya (Persona 4):** Likes her. Sees her as a future Director of Cat Captaincy. Encourages her to push Marcus.

## What success looks like for Diane in 12 months

- North America revenue at or above plan ($870M+).
- Louisiana share recovered.
- 2 NAM successions completed cleanly.
- A platform consolidation that her team thanks her for.
- Sophie graduated, Dylan thriving.
