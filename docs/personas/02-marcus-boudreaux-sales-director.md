## Who he is

Marcus is exactly the kind of CPG sales director Acme cannot afford to lose. He was raised in Lafayette, LA, played football at LSU, and has spent his entire adult life in CPG. He knows every regional buyer in his territory by first name, kid's name, and college team. He has been on a fishing boat in Lake Pontchartrain with the Rouses Markets head buyer. He has been the "Acme guy" at H-E-B for nine years.

He is charismatic, gregarious, and a force of nature in a room. He is also smart in a way that doesn't show up in his vocabulary — he can read a buyer's body language and know within 90 seconds whether the line review will go well. He hates that this skill is not legible to anyone in Battle Creek.

He is currently on the hottest seat in the company. The Q1 2026 Louisiana decline lands in his region, on his number, with his name on the variance report. Diane has not raised her voice but she has called him three times in the last week. Nathan has called twice. He is doing what he does — working the relationships, calling buyers, getting on planes — but he also knows this is a different kind of problem than the ones he is best at solving.

## Personality in three lines

- The friendliest person in the room and the most ruthless about his number. Both at the same time.
- Reads people, not dashboards. If you tell him a chart, he will tell you what the buyer thinks about it.
- Believes that everything in CPG is, ultimately, relationships and shelf space. He is mostly right.

## What Marcus is paid to do

Hit the South region revenue number. Defend distribution. Win line reviews. Develop NAM talent. Be the human face of Acme to ~30 retail accounts.

## His recurring pains

1. **Late-arriving data.** Sees Q1 share results 4 weeks after quarter-close. By the time he can act, the moment has passed.
2. **No early-warning signal.** The Louisiana decline was visible in week-over-week sell-through in November. Nobody synthesized it until April.
3. **Trade dollars are tighter than ever.** Marian Holt keeps trimming. The buyer keeps asking for more.
4. **Buyer relationships don't show up in the dashboard.** When he saves a distribution loss with a 30-minute fishing-boat conversation, no one in Battle Creek sees it.
5. **He is one degree of separation from every problem.** Hurricanes, DC delays, ERP issues, Walmart resets — all hit his number, none start in his control.
6. **The trade-pullback debate.** Marian and Whitfield want trade efficiency. Marcus wants enough trade to defend distribution. Both sides are right.
7. **The cereal-vs-adjacencies fight at HQ.** He has to sell ProteinPeak and RootDay into territories where his buyers don't believe in them yet, while Crunchwell is being asked to grow $0.

## What he'd never say out loud (but says privately)

- *"Half my job is convincing Battle Creek that the South is not a beta-test for everyone else's strategy."*
- *"I love Sage Park personally but ProteinPeak does not work in a Brookshire's in Tyler, Texas. Stop asking me to push it."*
- *"If I'd had this Louisiana data in November, we wouldn't be in this room today."*

## His core questions every Monday morning

- *"What is happening in my region this week that I need to be on top of?"*
- *"Where am I winning and where am I losing — by retailer, by SKU?"*
- *"What is Post / GM / private label doing right now in my territory?"*
- *"What's the line-review story I'll walk into Walmart with in August?"*
- *"What can I tell my buyer at Rouses on Friday that they don't already know?"*

## Tools he'd never give up

- His relationships. He'll resist any tool that he feels is "AI between me and the buyer."
- Walmart Luminate. Required tool, deeply embedded.
- His iPhone. He runs the region on text messages.

## What he wants from Clayface (without using the word "Clayface")

- *"Tell me what changed in my territory last week before someone in Battle Creek asks me about it."*
- *"Give me a one-pager I can hand to a buyer in Rouses that proves we are still the smart partner."*
- *"Show me where Post is winning so I can take it back."*
- *"Don't give me 50 dashboards — give me the 3 things I should walk into the buyer meeting with."*

## What loses Marcus in 60 seconds

- Anything that feels like Battle Creek tracking him
- "AI sales rep" framing — he will hang up
- A tool that requires him to type a query (he will not)
- A dashboard with more than 4 numbers on a page

## His relationship with the other three personas

- **With Maya (Persona 1):** Calls her for help; respects her quietly. Sometimes oversteps with weekend pings. Has talked her up to Nina twice.
- **With Diane (Persona 3):** His boss. Trusts her judgment. Believes she is the best leader he's worked under. Worries he's about to disappoint her.
- **With Priya (Persona 4):** Co-conspirator. They text constantly during line review prep. He calls her "the secret weapon."

## What success looks like for Marcus in 12 months

- Region revenue back on plan in H2 2026.
- Win back 2 facings of Crunchwell at Walmart in the August line review.
- A new RootDay distribution lane at H-E-B.
- His daughters healthy and his marriage intact through line review season.
