## Who she is

Maya is the kind of analyst every Insights team needs and almost no one trains. She is technically excellent — she can build a TURF analysis from scratch in R, and she has a sixth sense for when a syndicated number is wrong. She is also chronically overloaded: she supports the Crunchwell, HoneyNest, and (occasionally) ProteinPeak brands, sits on category-captaincy projects with Priya Raman, and gets pulled into ad-hoc requests from Diane Halverson's office two-to-three times per week.

She is quietly ambitious. She wants to be a Director by 35 and a Head of Insights by 40. She knows the path runs through visible work — the kind of slide that gets quoted in the Monday CEO read-out — but most of her week is spent assembling data, not synthesizing it. That gap is the source of her frustration and the place Clayface needs to land.

She is politically careful. She will not say "I think Marcus is wrong about the Louisiana root cause" in a meeting. She *will* prepare a one-pager that lets Diane reach that conclusion herself.

## Personality in three lines

- Composed under pressure. Sends the calmest Slack messages on her team at 9:47 PM on a Thursday.
- Deep but not loud. Speaks in meetings only when she has something worth saying; when she does, the room listens.
- Trusts the data more than the executive suite. She has lost arguments to gut-feel before; she still does the work anyway.

## What Maya is paid to do

Take a fuzzy question from a stakeholder, turn it into evidence, build the narrative, hand it back. The faster, the better. The more defensible, the better. The more reusable across stakeholders, the better.

## Her recurring pains

1. **Schema drift across tools.** Every retailer codes the cereal category slightly differently. Reconciling Nielsen Connect with Walmart Luminate consumes ~6 hours per week.
2. **The "by Friday" problem.** Most stakeholder asks land Monday and need answers by Friday business review. Insufficient time for primary research; over-reliance on syndicated data.
3. **Story-building, not data-pulling.** She spends 60% of her time pulling, 25% formatting, 15% synthesizing. She wants to invert that.
4. **Repeating herself.** The same Louisiana question will be asked five different ways by five different stakeholders. She rebuilds the deck five times.
5. **Tool sprawl.** 11 tools in active use. She has the muscle memory but the cognitive switching cost is real.
6. **Being right and unheard.** She flagged the Walmart facing reduction's downstream risk in October 2025. The deck went into a folder. Six months later it's a board issue.
7. **The ProteinPeak-Crunchwell tension.** Cory Whitman and Sage Park both ask for her time and treat her like she works for them. She technically works for neither; she works for Audrey via Robert via Nina.

## What she'd never say out loud

- *"If I had two more hours per day I'd be a Director by now."*
- *"Half the questions I get asked have already been answered in last quarter's deck. They just don't search."*
- *"I think the Louisiana decline is 70% Walmart facings, 20% Larksfield promo, 10% noise — but if I say that without 8 charts, no one believes me."*

## Her core questions when she opens a tool on Monday morning

- *"What changed in Crunchwell sales last week, and where?"*
- *"Is anything anomalous in Louisiana that I should bring up before someone asks?"*
- *"What's Larksfield doing this week that I should know about?"*
- *"What slides do I have that already answer this question, that I can reuse?"*
- *"How do I get a defensible answer by Friday for Diane?"*

## Tools she'd happily kill (if a single platform replaced them)

Tastewise, Brandwatch (if integrated social/creator coverage exists), Suzy (if ad-hoc consumer queries are integrated), and a few of her custom Tableau dashboards that she rebuilds every quarter.

## What would make her champion Clayface

- A Monday morning briefing — "here's what changed, here's the chart, here's the so-what" — that lets her open her week ahead instead of behind.
- Re-usable threads: ask a question once, turn it into a slide, share the slide, get a follow-up answer in the same thread.
- Schema-aware joins between Nielsen, Luminate, Stratum, Numerator without Maya doing the reconciliation.
- An export-to-PowerPoint flow that respects Acme's brand template.
- A way to get a sourced answer in 4 minutes that today takes 90.

## What would lose her in 30 seconds

- A homepage with 50 cards
- "AI brand manager" framing — she has heard it from three vendors and it is condescending
- Charts that she can't recreate in Tableau (because she will be asked for the source)
- Recommendations without traceable evidence
