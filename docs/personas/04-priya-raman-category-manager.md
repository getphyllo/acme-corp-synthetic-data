## Who she is

Priya is the most data-fluent person on the Sales side of Acme. She sits at the seam between Sales (her boss Diane), Insights (her dotted-line manager Nina), Marketing (she partners with the Brand teams on positioning), and the retailers (she is the "category captain" at Kroger and "category advisor" at Walmart, which means she helps the retailer think about the entire cereal aisle, not just Acme's brands).

She is tactical and analytical in equal measure. She can build a price-elasticity curve for Crunchwell SKUs in a 4-hour Saturday morning while sourdough proofs. She can walk into a Kroger category review and present a 47-slide deck without notes. She is also a working mother of two young kids, and her week is a constant balancing act between intensive line-review prep and parent-teacher conferences.

She is in a high-leverage role. The August Walmart line review and the November Kroger line review will both be made or broken by her work. The Louisiana decline has pulled her into a fast-cycle workstream where she is partnering with Marcus and Maya on the recovery plan.

Internally, she is widely respected as someone who "doesn't waste your time." She doesn't do meetings without an agenda. She doesn't send 9-paragraph emails. She is the easiest person at Acme to work with — and the easiest to mistake for someone with less seniority because she does not push her opinions in the room. She just builds the deck that makes the right decision obvious.

## Personality in three lines

- Tactical. The opposite of strategic-is-vague — she'll convert any strategic question into a measurable one in 90 seconds.
- Brain like a switchboard. Holds Walmart, Kroger, H-E-B, and Costco contexts in parallel without confusing them.
- Quietly the most influential operator in Sales. Marcus calls her "the secret weapon." Diane is grooming her for a Director role.

Build the commercial story for cereals and adjacencies at major retailers. Run cat-captain meetings at Kroger and cat-advisor meetings at Walmart. Build line-review decks. Optimize the trade-promo calendar (in partnership with Marian Holt). Bridge between Sales (Marcus, Tom, Soo-jin), Insights (Maya), and Brand (Cory, Sage, Hannah). Hire and grow her two analysts.

## Her recurring pains

1. **The data reconciliation tax.** Walmart Luminate, Kroger Stratum, Numerator, and Acme's internal sales report all use slightly different category definitions. She loses 6+ hours a week reconciling.
2. **JDA + analytics gap.** JDA Space Planning gives her shelf modeling but not consumer/competitor synthesis. She has to maintain two parallel mental models.
3. **The 12-week prep cycle.** Every line review needs a 12-week prep window. With 4 majors per year (Walmart Aug, Kroger Nov, Target Mar, Costco rolling), she is in line-review-prep mode 30+ weeks a year.
4. **Brand team asks vs. Sales team asks.** Cory wants the line-review pitch to lead with brand health. Marcus wants it to lead with shelf velocity. She has to bridge.
5. **Working mother math.** Her kids are 7 and 4. Her job has 12-week prep cycles. The math doesn't always work; she makes it work.
6. **Career visibility.** She is the most influential Director-level person at Acme that Whitfield has never had a 1:1 with. Diane has flagged this — change is coming.

## What she wants from a Clayface-shaped tool

- *"Show me share-of-shelf, share-of-voice, and share-of-velocity in one place — for cereal at Walmart, Kroger, HEB, Target — by SKU."*
- *"Tell me what changed in the Kroger cereal aisle this week, with the competitor activity overlaid."*
- *"Build me an exec slide that says 'Crunchwell deserves these 2 facings back' with the consumer-demand evidence pre-formatted."*
- *"Tie my consumer-demand argument to the Brand-equity story Cory wants to tell, in one deck, that I can present at Walmart and at Kroger with 80% reuse."*
- *"Don't make me reconcile Nielsen and Luminate and Stratum every Tuesday. Do it for me."*

## How she shows up in a buying cycle

- Critical pull-through user, particularly for line-review prep.
- Will engage in the deepest technical demo of any persona except possibly Maya.
- Will give an opinion on whether the platform's category definitions match how Walmart and Kroger actually code the category. (If they don't, she'll know within 5 minutes.)

## What loses Priya

- Generic cereal-category demos. She has built half the cereal-category data products in the industry. Show her something specific.
- Anything that abstracts away the retailer-specific reality (Walmart cereal aisle != Kroger cereal aisle).
- Slow exports. If she can't get a chart into a PPT in 30 seconds, she'll go back to Tableau.
