## Headline

**Acme's Crunchwell brand share in the Louisiana DMA fell 340 basis points in the 12-week period ending March 29, 2026 (vs. the same period prior year).** This is the largest single-DMA share decline Acme has experienced in the cereal core in the last six fiscal years. The decline is concentrated in Crunchwell Original Mega and Crunchwell Honey Nut Mega, in the New Orleans–Metairie and Baton Rouge MSAs, in the Mass and Grocery channels.

The decline is **not random**. It is the compounding effect of four root causes, three of which were addressable earlier than they were addressed.

It is a distribution issue compounded by competitive promo and a supply disruption.

## Root cause analysis — the five hypotheses

### H1 — Walmart Sept 2025 modular reset (PRIMARY DRIVER · ~55% of impact)

**What happened:** In September 2025, Walmart's annual cereal modular reset reduced Crunchwell facings from 8 to 6 across most divisions. The four affected SKUs were Original Mega, Honey Nut Mega, Multigrain, and Cinnamon Twist. The South division was hit harder than average — Walmart's local merchandising team applied a "regional sweetened cereal demand index" that under-weighted Crunchwell's actual velocities.

**Evidence:**
- Walmart Luminate facing audit confirms 6 facings at 84% of LA Walmart stores (Q1 2026), down from 8 facings at 91% (FY24).
- Velocity per facing on Crunchwell Mega in LA Walmart stores actually *increased* 4% — meaning consumers wanted the product but couldn't reach it because of OOS.
- Out-of-stock rate on Crunchwell Mega in LA Walmart spiked from 4.1% (FY24 avg) to 11.8% (Q1 2026).

**Why it compounded in LA specifically:** Walmart's South division uses a smaller modular for Crunchwell than the national average, so a 2-facing reduction is proportionally more painful in the South. Also, Walmart represents ~38% of LA cereal volume — a higher Walmart concentration than the national average (31%).

**Owner of fix:** Tom Reilly (NAM Walmart) — August 2026 line review. Acme is asking for 2 facings back on Original Mega and Honey Nut Mega, supported by velocity-per-facing data and the Crunchwell pack refresh as a "consumer demand catalyst."

### H2 — Post Foods aggressive promo intensification at Rouses & Brookshire's (SECONDARY DRIVER · ~20% of impact)

**What happened:** Beginning in late October 2025, Post Foods (specifically Honey Bunches of Oats and Great Grains) ran an aggressive 14-week promotional cadence at Rouses Markets and Brookshire's stores in LA. Promotion depth: $0.50 off the 14oz Honey Bunches of Oats (vs. Acme's $0.40 off); 4-week endcap at Rouses; trade-funded display flag.

**Evidence:**
- Numerator panel data shows 11% increase in Honey Bunches of Oats household reach in LA Q4 2025 → Q1 2026.
- Switching analysis: in Q1 2026, 14% of LA Crunchwell lapsed buyers bought Honey Bunches of Oats at next purchase, vs. 6% LA baseline (FY24).
- Rouses category buyer (per Marcus's May 11 conversation) confirmed Post's trade depth was "a half step ahead" of Acme.

**Why it compounded:** Acme's trade-pullback strategy on Crunchwell (26% → 22% trade depth target) coincided exactly with Post's intensification. Acme blinked first.

**Owner of fix:** Marcus Boudreaux + Marian Holt + Diane Halverson — proposed targeted 4-week trade injection at Rouses ($0.55 off) to close the depth gap, with hard end date.

### H3 — Hurricane Tonya disruption to Houston DC (TERTIARY DRIVER · ~12% of impact)

**What happened:** Hurricane Tonya made landfall near Beaumont, TX on November 8, 2025. Acme's Houston DC was offline for 9 days for safety inspection, then ran at 60% capacity for an additional 18 days as the regional power grid recovered. Crunchwell Mega specifically — produced in Lancaster PA, shipped through Houston DC for South region — experienced 4-week stockout cycles at Rouses, Brookshire's, and select LA Walmart stores extending into early January.

**Evidence:**
- DC outbound shipment data shows Crunchwell Mega volume to LA stores down 47% during the Nov 9–Dec 10 window.
- Customer service tickets logged 23 OOS escalations from LA stores in November–December 2025.
- Digital shelf data (Walmart.com search-rank for Crunchwell Mega in LA ZIP codes) shows substitution behavior — when OOS, consumers were served Cheerios and Honey Bunches of Oats as recommendations.

**Why it compounded:** The OOS coincided with Post's promo cadence. Consumers who couldn't find Crunchwell tried Honey Bunches of Oats on promo and stuck. Switching data confirms.

**Owner of fix:** Already addressed operationally; supply restored by mid-January 2026. Long-tail repeat behavior is the residual issue.

### H4 — Hispanic household demographic shift (CONTEXTUAL · ~5% of impact)

**What happened:** Louisiana's Hispanic population grew 9% in 2024–2025 (Census ACS). Hispanic households over-index on sweetened corn-flake formats (Maizoro, Maizena) which Acme does not produce. Crunchwell does not over-index in this demographic.

**Evidence:**
- Numerator panel shows Hispanic HH cereal repertoire skews toward corn-flake formats by 2.4× vs. non-Hispanic households.
- Acme's Crunchwell Hispanic HH penetration in LA: 4.1% vs. non-Hispanic 11.7%.

**Why it's contextual, not causal:** The demographic shift is real but slow (9% population growth over 2 years). It cannot mathematically account for a 340bps share loss in 12 weeks. It is a 12-month story, not a 12-week story.

**Owner of long-term opportunity:** Lillian Park / Innovation team — under-investigated. Considering a Hispanic-format flanker for FY27.

### H5 — Walmart Great Value private label growth (CONTEXTUAL · ~8% of impact)

**What happened:** Walmart Great Value Honey Toasted Oats (private label Cheerios analog) gained 130bps in LA Walmart stores in Q1 2026, partly from Crunchwell's lost facings being filled with PL.

**Evidence:**
- Walmart Luminate shows Great Value Honey Toasted Oats facings *increased* by 2 in the same modular where Crunchwell *lost* 2.
- Price gap to Crunchwell Mega: $1.40 cheaper per 14oz unit.

**Why it's contextual:** Great Value growth is a downstream effect of Walmart's reset — not an independent root cause.

## What would have caught this earlier

This is the most uncomfortable section of the report. Each of these signals existed; none of them were synthesized.

| Signal | When visible | When acted on | Lag |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Walmart facing reset risk flagged | Aug 2025 (modular preview) | Mar 2026 (this report) | **7 months** |
| Post promo intensification at Rouses | Oct 2025 (NielsenIQ promo data) | May 2026 | **7 months** |
| Houston DC OOS extending past supply restoration | Jan 2026 (customer service tickets) | Mar 2026 | **2 months** |
| Switching from Crunchwell → Honey Bunches in LA panel | Dec 2025 (Numerator) | Mar 2026 | **3 months** |
| Q4 2025 LA share down 110bps (early warning) | Jan 2026 (NielsenIQ Q4 read) | Mar 2026 (Q1 alarm) | **2 months** |

**The honest read:** every individual signal was inside a tool Acme already pays for. No one synthesized them across tools, across functions, across geographies, or across timeframes. The first time a single human had all five signals on one page was when Maya Chen built the hypothesis tree on May 12, 2026 — six weeks after the worst of the damage was done.

## Recovery plan — three-leg approach

### Leg 1 — Tactical trade response at Rouses (immediate, 4 weeks)

- **Action:** $0.55 off 14oz Crunchwell Mega and Honey Nut Mega at Rouses for 4 weeks beginning May 25, 2026.
- **Cost:** ~$280K trade investment.
- **Expected lift:** Recapture ~120bps of LA share by end of June.
- **Risk:** Sets a depth precedent — Marian Holt is uncomfortable. Mitigated by hard end date and explicit "tactical recovery" framing in JBP language.
- **Owner:** Marcus Boudreaux. Sign-off: Diane Halverson, Marian Holt.

### Leg 2 — Walmart facing recovery (medium, August line review)

- **Action:** August 2026 line-review pitch to add back 2 facings on Crunchwell Mega and Honey Nut Mega. Anchor on velocity-per-facing data + pack refresh launch.
- **Expected lift:** ~150–180bps if Walmart approves all 2 facings; ~70–90bps if only 1.
- **Owner:** Tom Reilly + Priya Raman. Sign-off: Diane.

### Leg 3 — Supply assurance + brand-equity narrative (ongoing)

- **Action 1:** Houston DC contingency planning for hurricane season 2026 — backup pre-positioning at Memphis 3PL.
- **Action 2:** Crunchwell pack refresh launch supported with $1.4M LA-targeted media in Q3 2026 (Audrey + Hugo Lin owned).
- **Expected lift:** ~50–80bps of LA share recovered by Q4 2026.
- **Owner:** Devraj (supply); Audrey + Hugo (media).
